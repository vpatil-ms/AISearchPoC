Setting Goals

Make sure the criteria you use for performance assessments are valid, job-related, and applied consistently. To be effective and meaningful, performance goals should be “SMART” (specific, measurable, attainable, relevant, and timely):

SMART

Ask yourself:

Example:

Specific

Are the goals clear with a desired outcome?

Raise awareness in the market and increase customer base by 10% using social media and email marketing campaigns

Measurable

How will you know when objectives are met? What criteria will you use to measure success?

When new customer base increases by 10% over last year

Attainable

Does the employee have the ability, time, resources, and support to achieve the goal?

The company just purchased a new email marketing program and the employee has a certificate in social media marketing

Relevant

Are objectives related to the employee's job? Do they serve the interests of the company?

The employee's job is marketing manager and because of the startup nature of the business, new customer acquisition is critical

Timely

What is the target date for measuring results?

12 months

To increase engagement and commitment, involve employees in the goal-setting process.

Note: In some cases, goals may need adjustment during the review period. For example, various federal, state, and local laws provide job-protected leave to employees. If an employee takes leave under one of these laws, performance expectations should be adjusted to account for the leave. For instance, if an employee is expected to make 100 sales within six months, but takes three months of job-protected leave, their sales target should be reduced to 50.
