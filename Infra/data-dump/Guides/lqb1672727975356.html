Overview

Background checks can provide employers with a variety of job-related information to use when evaluating prospective and current employees' qualifications, such as work history and education, criminal records, driving records, and credit history.

However, federal, state, and local laws set limits on obtaining and using background check information to make employment decisions. Employers must ensure compliance with these laws.

Note: This guide and the information available through it do not, and are not intended to, constitute legal advice. Consult legal counsel for any specific questions about background checks.
