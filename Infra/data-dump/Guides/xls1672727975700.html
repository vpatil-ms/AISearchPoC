OSH Act Overview

The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), an agency of the U.S. Department of Labor, enforces the Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) Act of 1970. OSHA aids employers and employees in decreasing the number of injuries, illnesses, and deaths in the workplace. The OSH Act covers all private-sector employers and their employees in all 50 U.S. states and all territories and jurisdictions under federal authority.

Specifically, the OSH Act spells out employees' rights and protections in regards to safety and health in the workplace. The Act also emphasizes employees' rights to file a complaint if they believe that the workplace is unsafe or unhealthy without fear of reprisal. All employees are protected from employer retaliation when reporting alleged safety and health violations or when taking part in OSHA inspections or hearings.
