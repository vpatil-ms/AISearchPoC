E-Verify - What is it?

E-Verify is a free Internet-based system operated by the Department of Homeland Security in partnership with the Social Security Administration. It allows employers to electronically verify the employment eligibility of newly hired employees by comparing the information provided on the employee's I-9 Form to Department of Homeland Security databases. These databases contain records about employment-based visas, immigration and naturalization status, and U.S. passport issuance, which allows E-Verify to compare information against a wide variety of sources.

While many employers are choosing to use the program voluntarily, several states require e-verification. See our chart on E-Verify Requirements by State for more information.

Visit the USCIS website for more information on the key features of E-Verify and to learn how to enroll.
