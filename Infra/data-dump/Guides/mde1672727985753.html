Second Injury Fund

If an employee has a serious, pre-existing condition that still allows the applicant to work, an employer may be reluctant to hire the person in case of a subsequent injury on top of the earlier condition. In an effort to discourage employers from discriminating against applicants who have suffered prior injuries, many states have created a second injury fund that pays a certain portion of the compensation benefits due to a re-injured employee.

Many employers question whether or not they can be reimbursed from the fund after paying full benefits to a re-injured employee if the employer was unaware of the injury in the first place. Some state courts have ruled that the employee's prior injury must be manifest for the employer to recover from the fund; in other words, the employer must have actual knowledge of the prior injury, or the prior injury must be obvious or officially documented and available to the employer. To avoid any such complications, many employers require that new employees undergo medical examinations prior to being hired.
