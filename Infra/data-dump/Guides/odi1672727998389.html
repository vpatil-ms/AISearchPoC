Bona Fide Occupational Qualifications (BFOQs)

Federal EEO laws prohibit employers from making employment decisions based on a protected characteristic, such as age, sex, or religion. There is a very narrow exception when the employer can show that the otherwise protected characteristic is a bona fide occupational qualification (BFOQ) reasonably necessary to the normal operation of that particular business or enterprise.

Note: Race is never a BFOQ.

In order to apply the BFOQ, a business must prove that no member of the excluded group can perform the job because of the otherwise protected characteristic. This is very difficult to prove—employment counsel should be consulted before attempting to apply a BFOQ.
