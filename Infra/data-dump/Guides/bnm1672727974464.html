Interviewing Overview

Interviews are an essential part of hiring process, helping you gather job-related information that you typically can't find through resumes and applications alone. Interviews can range from the traditional one-on-one interview to group interviews with multiple candidates. To narrow down the field, many employers set up multiple rounds of interviews, such as:

  • Phone Interviews: Phone interviews are generally relatively short (15-30 minutes) and should focus on clarifying some of the questions you might have after reviewing the candidate's resume, application form, or other pre-screening information.
  • Video Interviews: Video interviews are another option to consider prior to in-person interviews and can be especially beneficial if there are geographic limitations that make it challenging to schedule an in-person meeting. Video interviews can also help the interviewer to pick up on nonverbal cues (e.g., eye contact, level of interest, energy, and overall poise) that may not have been evident during a phone interview.
  • In-person Interviews: Consider inviting candidates that have successfully completed the phone and video interviews to come in for a face-to-face meeting.
