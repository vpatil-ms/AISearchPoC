Recordkeeping Overview

Properly maintaining employee records is important for a variety of reasons: records can help you to administer your company's human resource policies with greater efficiency; they can help demonstrate compliance with government recordkeeping and reporting requirements; and they can provide documentation to drive, and even defend, hiring, termination, discipline, compensation, and other related decisions.

There is more to the recordkeeping process than filing some employee forms in a manila folder. Most records must be kept in a secure location, accessible only by authorized personnel, and separate from other types of records. Additionally, there are a variety of employment laws that impact the recordkeeping process.
