Executive Order 13706 requires certain employers that contract with the federal government to provide their employees with up to seven days of paid sick leave annually. The requirements of the Executive Order apply only to certain categories of contracts and only to new contracts or replacements for expiring contracts on or after January 1, 2017.

Note: Some states and several local jurisdictions have their own paid sick leave requirements. In these locations, contractors will need to coordinate compliance with Executive Order 13706 and their state or local rule.

Covered Contracts:

Executive Order 13706 applies to the following contracts that are new or replacements for expiring contracts on or after January 1, 2017:

  • Procurement contracts for construction covered by the Davis-Bacon Act (DBA);
  • Service contracts covered by the McNamara-O'Hara Service Contract Act (SCA);
  • Concessions contracts, including any concessions contracts excluded from the SCA by the Department of Labor's regulations at 29 CFR 4.133(b);
  • Contracts in connection with federal property or lands and related to offering services for federal employees, their dependents, or the general public;
  • Any subcontract of a covered contract that (like the upper-tier contract) falls into one of the above categories.

Accrual and Carryover:

Employees of covered contractors accrue one hour of paid sick leave for every 30 hours worked on or in connection with a covered contract. Contractors may cap accrual at 56 hours per year and prohibit employees from having more than 56 hours of accrued leave available for use at any point in time. Contractors are permitted to frontload paid sick leave at the beginning of each accrual year instead of using an accrual method, as long as they frontload at least 56 hours. Contractors must allow employees to carryover unused paid sick leave to the following year, even when the leave is frontloaded.

Notice of Available Leave:

Contractors must notify employees in writing of the amount of paid sick leave they have available, at the end of each pay period or each month, whichever interval is shorter.

Use:

Paid sick leave may be used for the following purposes:

  • Physical or mental illness, injury, or medical condition;
  • To obtain diagnosis, care, or preventive care from a health care provider;
  • To care for the employee's child, parent, spouse, domestic partner, or any other individual related by blood or affinity whose close association with the employee is the equivalent of a family relationship who has any of the conditions described above, or needs diagnosis, care, or preventive care;
  • For medical care, counseling, relocation, or to seek assistance from a victim services organization, take legal action, or assist a covered family member in engaging in any of these activities when the employee or his or her family member is a victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking.

Note: Under the rule, contractors are only required to allow employees to use paid sick leave at times the employees would be working on a covered contract.

Pay and Benefits:

When employees use paid sick leave, contractors must provide them with the same regular pay and benefits they would have received if they had not used the leave.

Employee Notice:

Employees may request to use paid sick leave orally or in writing. A leave request must be made at least seven calendar days in advance where the need for the leave is foreseeable, and in other cases as soon as is practical.

Documentation:

For absences of three or more consecutive full days, a contractor may require documentation that the absence was for a covered purpose, as long as the contractor notifies the employee of the documentation requirement before he or she returns to work.
