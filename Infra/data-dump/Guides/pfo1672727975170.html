Overview

“Equal employment opportunity” or “EEO” is a term used to describe employment practices that aim to ensure nondiscrimination and equal access to work-related opportunities. Federal EEO laws, which are enforced by the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), prohibit discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, genetic information, age or disability. Virtually all employers must comply.

Due to significant changes in the nation's demographics, nearly every employer is facing an increasingly diverse workforce. Understanding your requirements as they relate to EEO laws will become increasingly essential in today's labor market.

Some states have their own EEO laws that provide for greater employee protections. For example, discrimination with regards to gender identity and sexual orientation is prohibited in certain states. Employers should check their state-specific requirements in order to ensure compliance and to provide for appropriate employee protections.
