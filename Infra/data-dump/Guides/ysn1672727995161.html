VEVRAA: Affirmative Action Programs

VEVRAA requires employers with 50 or more employees and a contract of $150,000 or more to comply with the following requirements:

  • Maintain a written affirmative action program. Covered contractors must prepare and maintain an affirmative action program at each establishment. This program may be integrated into or kept separate from other affirmative action programs. It must be reviewed and updated annually.
  • Make the affirmative action program available. The full affirmative action program must be available to any applicant or employee for inspection upon request. The location and hours during which the program may be obtained must be posted at each establishment.
  • Invite voluntary self-identification of protected veteran status. Covered contractors must invite applicants to voluntarily self-identify as a protected veteran at the pre-offer stage of the hiring process and after receiving a job offer.
  • Establish benchmark for hiring. Contractors must establish benchmarks to create a quantifiable method by which the contractor can measure its progress toward achieving equal employment opportunity for protected veterans. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.45.

VEVRAA's required elements for the affirmative action program are similar to those required by Section 503 and may be found at 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.44(a) through 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.44(j).
