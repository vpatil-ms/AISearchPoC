Voting Leave

Several jurisdictions have voting leave laws. While each law is different, typically they address:

  • When an employee can take leave to vote. Some states only permit leave if the employee doesn't have sufficient time outside of work hours to vote. Some laws allow employers to limit leave to the beginning or end of an employee's shift.
  • Pay. Most states require employees to be paid for this time.
  • Employer notice requirements. Some states, including California and New York, require employers to post a notice in the workplace informing employees of their right to take leave in advance of each major election.
  • Employee notice requirements. In some states, employees must provide, and employers can request, advance notice of the need for voting leave.
