Job Descriptions Overview

A job description is a written statement that describes the purpose of a job, its essential and nonessential functions, and the necessary skills and qualifications to perform the job. It's a best practice to have a written job description for each job in your company.

Job descriptions serve multiple functions. For hiring purposes, employers use them to identify the essential functions of the job and the qualifications needed for the role. Job descriptions also help set clear expectations for employees. Additionally, employers can refer to job descriptions to assess performance, make compensation decisions, and identify training needs. Using job descriptions to clearly define essential job functions can also help make appropriate accommodations, when necessary.

Key components of a job description include:

  • Job identification and job summary
  • Essential functions
  • Qualifications and Skills
  • Physical Demands and Work Environment
  • Statement about Scope and Changes

For job advertisements, employers can also include a section with the salary range for the position (some jurisdictions require this information in job advertisements) and an equal employment opportunity statement.
