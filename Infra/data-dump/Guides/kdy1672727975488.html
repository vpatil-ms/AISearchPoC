Hiring Overview

Hiring the right person is one of the most important decisions your company can make. That's why it's important to have a process in place to identify the most qualified applicants.

In general, the hiring process involves the steps shown here:

  • Determine staffing needs: Determine the reason for the opening and the type of employee that would best fit those needs (i.e., full-time, part-time, temporary, etc.).
  • Analyze and evaluate the job: Identify key attributes of the job, develop a job description, and create an effective job ad.
  • Recruit candidates: Create and post job ads, network, and/or seek talent internally.
  • Pre-screen candidates: Using resumes, employment applications, and pre-screening questions, evaluate applicants' employment history, experience, and qualifications.
  • Conduct interviews: Ask questions about their job-related skills, experience, and qualifications.
  • Select a candidate: Determine which candidate is best suited for the job based on the information collected.
  • Extend an offer: Extend a conditional job offer to the candidate.
  • Complete background check and other pre-employment testing: After obtaining authorization from the candidate, conduct job-related background checks, reference checks or other pre-employment testing in accordance with applicable law.
  • Onboard the new hire: Get the employee started on the right foot.

    This guide and the information available through it do not, and are not intended to, constitute legal advice. Consult legal counsel for any specific questions about hiring.
