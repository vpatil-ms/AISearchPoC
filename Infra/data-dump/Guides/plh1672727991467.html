Section 503: Affirmative Action Programs

Under Section 503, contractors with 50 or more employees and government contracts of $50,000 or more must:

  • Maintain a written AAP. Covered contractors must prepare and maintain a written affirmative action program at each establishment. This program may be integrated into or kept separate from other affirmative action programs. It must be reviewed and updated annually.
  • Make the AAP available. The full affirmative action program must be available to any applicant or employee for inspection upon request. The location and hours during which program details may be obtained must be posted at each establishment.
  • Invite voluntary self-identification of disability. Covered contractors must invite applicants to voluntarily self-identify as an individual with a disability at the pre-offer stage of the hiring process and after receiving a job offer. Contractors must invite existing employees to voluntarily self-identify on a regular basis.
  • Adopt utilization goal. The OFCCP has established a national seven percent utilization goal for the employment of qualified individuals with disabilities. The purpose of the goal is to establish a benchmark against which the contractor must measure the representation of individuals with disabilities in each of its job groups. Contractors must conduct an annual utilization analysis to compare the representation of individuals with disabilities in each job group to the seven percent utilization goal. However, contractors with 100 or fewer employees may apply the goal to their workforce as a whole.

Required Components:

Section 503 requires that affirmative action programs contain the following elements:

Policy Statement

Among other things, contractors must include an equal opportunity policy statement in their affirmative action program and post the policy statement on company bulletin boards. Contractors must ensure that applicants and employees with disabilities are provided the notice in a form that is accessible and understandable to the individual with a disability. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(a).

Review of Personnel Process

Contractors must review their personnel processes to, among other things, ensure they provide for careful, thorough, and systematic consideration of the job qualifications of applicants and employees with known disabilities. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(b).

Review of Physical and Mental Job Qualifications

Contractors must review all physical and mental job qualification standards to ensure that they are job-related for the position in question and are consistent with business necessity. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(c).

Reasonable Accommodations

Contractors must provide reasonable accommodations for the known physical or mental limitations of an otherwise qualified individual with a disability unless they can demonstrate that the accommodation would impose an undue hardship on the operation of their business. The regulations also recommend that the contractor establish written procedures for handling requests for reasonable accommodations. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(d).

Anti-harassment

Contractors must develop and implement procedures to ensure that its employees are not harassed on the basis of disability.

Recruitment

Contractors must engage in appropriate outreach and recruitment activities that are reasonably designed to effectively recruit qualified individuals with disabilities. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(f).

Dissemination of policy

Contractors must develop procedures to communicate its obligation to engage in affirmative action efforts internally. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-741.44(g).

Audit and Reporting System

Contractors must design and implement an audit and reporting system that will:

  • Measure the effectiveness of the program;
  • Identify any need for remedial action;
  • Determine the degree to which the contractor's objectives have been met;
  • Determine whether individuals with disabilities have had the opportunity to participate in all company sponsored educational, training, recreational, and social activities;
  • Measure the contractor's compliance with the affirmative action program's specific obligations; and
  • Document the actions taken to comply with the auditing and reporting obligations above, and retain these records.

Responsibility for Implementation

Contractors must assign the responsibility for implementation of affirmative action activities to a designated official.

Training

All employees involved in the recruitment, screening, selection, promotion, disciplinary, and related processes must be trained to ensure that the commitments in the contractor's affirmative action program are implemented.

Data Collection Analysis

Contractors must calculate and document on an annual basis the following information and maintain it for a period of three years:

  • The number of applicants who self-identified as individuals with disabilities, or who are otherwise known to be individuals with disabilities;
  • The total number of job openings and total number of jobs filled;
  • The total number of applicants for all jobs;
  • The number of applicants with disabilities hired; and
  • The total number of applicants hired.
