Maintaining and Updating an Employee Handbook

Regularly updating your employee handbook can help to ensure that it remains compliant with applicable laws and that it is still meeting your needs. Below are some guidelines for revising and maintaining your employee handbook:

Establish an approval process:

Determine who is authorized to make changes to the employee handbook and who must approve the changes.

Inform employees of any changes:

Determine how you will communicate any handbook changes to your employees. Try to give employees as much notice as possible before implementing policy changes. Consider multiple methods for communicating changes, such as email, memos, notices in common areas, and discussions at staff meetings. Then, ask employees to sign another acknowledgment form verifying they have read and understand the changes.

Monitor changes:

Review your handbook periodically to ensure company practices are aligned with company policy, and to ensure that policies are consistent with applicable laws. Laws change regularly, so be aware of how these changes can affect company policies and practices.
