Duration of COBRA Continuation Coverage

Depending on the qualifying event, COBRA continuation coverage must be provided from the date of the qualifying event, for a period of 18, 29 or 36 months. A Plan Administrator can elect to measure the maximum period of COBRA continuation coverage from the date coverage is lost. A group health plan, however, may provide longer periods of coverage beyond the maximum required by COBRA.

18 Months:

When the qualifying event is the covered employee's termination of employment (for reasons other than gross misconduct) or reduction in hours of work to fewer than the number required for plan participation, qualified beneficiaries must be provided a maximum of up to 18 months of COBRA continuation coverage.

36 Months:

For all other qualifying events (besides termination and reduction in hours), qualified beneficiaries must be provided up to 36 months of COBRA continuation coverage.

Note: There are a number of factors that must be considered when determining the maximum period for COBRA continuation coverage.

Extension of COBRA Continuation Coverage:

There are two circumstances under which COBRA continuation coverage can be extended: (1) when one of the qualified beneficiaries is disabled; (2) when a second qualifying event occurs.

Disability Extension:

If one of the qualified beneficiaries is disabled and meets certain requirements, all of the qualified beneficiaries in that family are entitled to an 11-month extension (for a total maximum period of up to 29 months) of COBRA continuation coverage.

Second Qualifying Event:

An 18-month extension may be available to qualified beneficiaries (who are the spouse or child of a covered employee) receiving an 18-month maximum period of COBRA continuation coverage (giving a total maximum period of up to 36 months of COBRA continuation coverage) if the qualified beneficiaries experience a second qualifying event that is: death of the covered employee, divorce or legal separation of the covered employee and spouse, covered employee's Medicare entitlement, or loss of dependent child status under the plan.

Note: The second qualifying event must occur while the spouse or child has COBRA continuation coverage. In addition, the first qualifying event must be a termination of employment or reduction of hours.

Early Termination of COBRA Continuation Coverage:

A group health plan may terminate COBRA continuation coverage early for any of the following reasons:

  • Premiums are not paid in full on a timely basis
  • The employer ceases to maintain any group health plan (including successor plans) for active employees
  • A qualified beneficiary begins coverage under another group health plan after electing COBRA continuation coverage
  • Loss of disability status of a qualified beneficiary during the period of a COBRA continuation coverage disability extension
  • A qualified beneficiary becomes entitled to Medicare benefits after electing COBRA continuation coverage
  • A qualified beneficiary engages in conduct that would justify terminating coverage of a similarly situated participant or beneficiary not receiving COBRA continuation coverage (such as fraud)

If COBRA continuation coverage is terminated early, the Plan Administrator must provide the qualified beneficiary with an early termination notice.
