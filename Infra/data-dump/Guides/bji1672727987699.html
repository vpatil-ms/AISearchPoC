Furloughs and Layoffs: What's the Difference?

The term “furlough” is loosely used to refer to a reduction in the number of days or weeks that an employee works because of a decline in business activity. In other words, the employee takes unpaid time off, usually for a short-term, and sometimes defined, period. By contrast, the term “layoff” is typically used by employers to refer to an indefinite long-term or permanent separation from employment. The key point to remember is that both furloughs and layoffs may trigger notice, pay, benefits, unemployment, and other compliance obligations. Check your state law for details.
