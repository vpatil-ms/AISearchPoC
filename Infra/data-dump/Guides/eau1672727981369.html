Pre-Handbook Development Steps

Before and during the handbook development phase, it's important to ask yourself the following questions:

Have you done your research?

Analyze the Company

To help ensure that your handbook accurately represents the company, investigate company history, practices and culture, and what current policies and procedures are most important to the business. This can help to determine the tone in which you write the handbook as well as which policies you include.

Analyze the Law

Research relevant employment laws to determine what to include in your handbook. This includes information related to equal employment opportunities, sexual harassment, leaves of absence, paid time off, and pay.

In addition, determine how state and local laws may impact the contents of your handbook. For instance, under federal law employers are prohibited from discriminating on the basis of: race, color, religion, sex, genetic information, national origin, age, or disability. However, some state and local laws also prohibit discrimination on other characteristics. When drafting these types of policies, be aware of all applicable requirements.
