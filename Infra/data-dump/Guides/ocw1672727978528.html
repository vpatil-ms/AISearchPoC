Glossary of Terms

The following is a glossary of terms related to COBRA:

  • COBRA Continuation Coverage: A temporary extension of group health plan coverage at group rates after certain events that would otherwise cause an individual to lose his or her group health plan coverage.
  • Qualifying Event: An event that causes an individual to lose group health plan coverage and trigger his or her right to elect COBRA continuation coverage. Examples of qualifying events include voluntary or involuntary job loss, reduction in the hours worked, divorce, and loss of dependent child status.
  • Qualified Beneficiary: An individual covered by a group health plan on the day before a qualifying event (a covered employee, the covered employee's spouse, or a covered employee's dependent child). Any child born to or placed for adoption with a covered employee during a period of COBRA continuation coverage is automatically considered a qualified beneficiary. Each of the qualified beneficiaries for a qualifying event may independently elect COBRA continuation coverage.
  • Dependent Child: A child who receives group health plan coverage under his or her parent's group health plan.
  • Election Period: A period in which Qualified Beneficiaries are allowed to choose whether to elect COBRA continuation coverage. The election period must end no earlier than the later of the following two dates:
    • 60 days after the date coverage would otherwise terminate under the plan because of the Qualifying Event; or
    • 60 days after the date the Qualified Beneficiary was notified of the right to elect COBRA Continuation Coverage
  • Plan Administrator: The individual or organization directly responsible for carrying out the requirements of COBRA. Some group health plans are administered by the employer, but most group health plans are administered by a third party.
  • COBRA General Notice: A notice that contains important information about COBRA continuation coverage. The notice must be given by the Plan Administrator to each covered employee and their spouse who becomes covered under the group health plan.
  • COBRA Election Notice: A notice that describes the rights to COBRA continuation coverage and how to make an election for such coverage. The election notice must be provided by the Plan Administrator to Qualified Beneficiaries following a qualifying event.
  • Multi-Employer Plan: Generally, an employee health plan maintained pursuant to a collective bargaining agreement that includes employees of two or more employers who are required to contribute to the plan. These plans are also known as jointly-administered plans.
