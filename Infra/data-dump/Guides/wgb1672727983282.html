Based on the job requirements, formulate a job ad aimed at attracting candidates that meet your needs.

Brief description of job and company:

Include a brief description of the job and its essential functions. Describe your industry, products and services, and the mission and goals of your company. Mentioning company perks, such as a competitive benefits package or flexible work hours, is also important for attracting candidates. If applicable, provide a link to your company's website and/or career page so candidates can learn more.

Location:

Including the location of the job will help candidates assess the feasibility of a commute to your business location.

Qualification requirements:

Job qualifications include the training, education, certification(s), and years of experience needed to perform the job. Differentiate between required qualifications and preferred qualifications. For example, “three years of industry experience is required; five years is preferred.”

Specify selection requirements:

Consider outlining your pre-employment screening requirements, such as drug screens or background checks. Make sure the inclusion of any such screening requirements complies with applicable laws. For example, some states and local jurisdictions prohibit or restrict employers from making certain statements about criminal history in job ads.

Salary range:

Indicating a salary range may help to limit your applicant pool by discouraging those with higher salary expectations. Note: Some states and local jurisdictions require employers to display the salary range for a position.

Instructions on how to apply:

Provide specific instructions on how to apply (e.g., online, via email, etc.).

“Equal Opportunity Employer” (EOE) Statement:

An EOE statement helps to demonstrate that you do not discriminate on the basis of any characteristics that are protected by law.
