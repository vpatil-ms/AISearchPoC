The Immigration Reform and Control Act (IRCA) requires employers to complete Form I-9 for each newly hired employee. Section 1 of the I-9 form must be completed on the employee's first day of work. Section 2, which requires employers to review and verify supporting documents, must be completed within three business days of the employee's start date.

Photocopies of Supporting Documents:

Some employers choose to photocopy employees' presented documents. Note that when an employer participates in E-Verify and the employee presents certain documents, the employer must retain a photocopy of the document. If you do decide to make photocopies of presented documents you must do so consistently for all employees. Photocopies may only be used for the verification process and should be retained with the employee's I-9.

Retention:

The completed I-9 form must be retained for three years following an employee's date of hire or one year following their termination, whichever is later. It is a best practice to store all I-9 forms together in one file so that they can be promptly produced following an official government request. I-9s must be produced within three days of such a request.

Rehired Employees:

If more than three years have passed since the rehired employee initially completed the I-9, he or she must complete a new I-9. Otherwise, you may re-verify their employment eligibility by completing Section 3 of the form. If the employee's original form of identification has expired, you will need to examine a document that reflects current employment eligibility and record the document title, number, and expiration date within this section.

Note: Current employees are not required to re-verify their employment eligibility or complete a new I-9 every three years unless their work authorization documents have expired (see below for more information the re-verification process).

Re-verification:

For employees who indicated an employment authorization expiration date in Section 1, employers are required to re-verify their employment eligibility on or before the date provided. Complete Section 3 when updating and/or re-verifying Form I-9.
