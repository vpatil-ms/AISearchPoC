OSHA has encouraged employees and employers alike to make occupational health and safety initiatives paramount. To this end, OSHA has created the following programs: the Voluntary Protection Program, the Strategic Partnership Program, and the Alliance Program.

Voluntary Protection Program:

OSHA's Voluntary Protection Program (VPP) provides an opportunity for labor, management, and government to work together cooperatively to further the goal of providing effective safety and health protection in the workplace. The VPP grants recognition to worksites that provide, or are committed to providing, effective protection for their employees through implementation of systematically managed safety and health programs.

All groups covered by OSHA are eligible to join the VPP. Each worksite that applies must show a commitment to effective safe and health management systems and work to be an industry leader in occupational safety and health.

There are three levels of recognition; each is designed with different criteria to allow the opportunity for a wide range of safety and health programs to be recognized.

  • Star Program.
  • Merit Program.
  • Demonstration Program.

OSHA Strategic Partnership Program:

The OSHA Strategic Partnership Program (OSPP) is an expansion and formalization of OSHA's experience with voluntary programs. The OSPP is designed to enable groups of employers, employees, and employee representatives to partner with OSHA and enter into an extended, voluntary, and cooperative relationship in order to encourage, assist, and recognize efforts to eliminate serious hazards and achieve a high level of worker safety and health.

OSHA Alliance Program:

Through the Alliance Program, OSHA works with groups committed to safety and health, including businesses, trade or professional organizations, unions and educational institutions.

Alliances focus on the following goals:

  • Training and education;
  • Outreach and communications;
  • Promoting the national dialogue on occupational safety and health.
