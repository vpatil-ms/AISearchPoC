FMLA Recordkeeping Requirements

The Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) requires covered employers (those with 50 or more employees) to maintain the following for at least three years:

  • Dates of FMLA leave
  • Hours of FMLA leave, if taken in increments of less than one full day
  • Copies of all notices provided to employees related to FMLA leave, including Designation Notices and Notices of Eligibility
  • Copies of requests for leave
  • Records of any disputes regarding the designation of leave as FMLA
  • FMLA-related medical records and documents relating to medical certifications, re-certifications, or medical histories of employees or employees' family members (when FMLA leave is taken for a serious health condition).

    Note: These records must be maintained in a confidential file, separate from personnel files.
