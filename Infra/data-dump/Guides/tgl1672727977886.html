Claim Procedure

Each employer should consult with their lawyer, insurance carrier, and state officials to determine their own liability. It is essential that employers designate one department or individual to be responsible for the administration of WC claims; failure to respond timely to injuries, claims, and government notices may result in validation of unwarranted claims and major expenses to the employer. Typically, this is how a claim procedure works:

  1. If an employee is injured on the job, he or she must notify the employer and then file a claim for compensation with the state agency that administers the compensation act.
  2. Once the employer learns of the compensable injury, the employer must file a report with the state agency as well. Employers or individuals responsible for handling matters of WC in a company must be aware of all time limits and deadlines.
  3. The employer or their insurance carrier must either begin paying benefits or deny the claim.
  4. If the claim is denied, the government agency investigates, conducts a hearing if necessary, and then either denies or awards coverage to an employee. If necessary, further appeal may be made to the courts.

All states have specific regulations regarding insurance payments for medical treatment at the time of injury, follow-up treatments, time-off for treatments, and some may even include transportation to and from treatments.
