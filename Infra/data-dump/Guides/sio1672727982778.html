Covered Group Healthcare Plans

COBRA only applies to group health plans. In general, an employer that does not sponsor a group health plan is not covered by COBRA. The law, however, defines group health plans broadly.

A group health plan is defined as any arrangement that an employer establishes or maintains to provide employees or their families with medical care, whether it is provided through insurance, by a health maintenance organization, out of the employer's assets, or through any other means.

Medical, dental and vision benefits are considered group health plans. Life insurance and disability benefits are not considered “medical care.”
