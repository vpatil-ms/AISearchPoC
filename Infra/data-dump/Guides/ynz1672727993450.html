Vietnam Era Veterans' Readjustment Assistance Act of 1974 (VEVRAA) prohibits federal contractors and subcontractors from discriminating in employment against protected veterans, and requires these employers to take affirmative action to recruit, hire, promote, and retain these veterans. VEVRAA applies to employers with federal contracts of $150,000 or more.

Basic Requirements:

EEO Clause

Contracts must state that the contractor will not discriminate against any applicant or employee because he or she is a protected veteran. A protected veteran is a disabled veteran, recently separated veteran, active duty wartime or campaign badge veteran, or Armed Forces service medal veteran. The contractor must also agree to take affirmative action to employ and advance protected veterans and to treat protected veterans without discrimination.

The contractor must also agree to list all employment openings (except for executive and top management positions, positions that will be filled internally, and positions lasting three days or less) with the appropriate employment service delivery system. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.5.

Recordkeeping

Generally, contractors must keep any personnel or employment record for two years from the date the record is created or the personnel action is taken, whichever occurs later. However, certain regulations require employers to maintain certain records relating to veterans and disability status for three years. Contractors that have fewer than 150 employees or that do not have a Government contract of at least $150,000 must keep such records for a period of one year. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.80.

Access to Records

Contractors must permit the OFCCP access to their places of business for the purpose of conducting on-site compliance evaluations and complaint investigations and inspecting and copying records. For more information, see 41 CFR Sec. 60-300.81.

Reporting

Once per year, between August 1 and September 30, contractors must complete and submit the VETS-4212 Report showing the numbers of protected veterans hired or employed during the reporting period.
