Performance appraisals, commonly referred to as performance reviews, are a formal way to evaluate employee performance during a standard review period, typically one year. However, some employers subject new employees to more frequent reviews. During the review, supervisors rate each employee on how successfully they performed a specific standard or goal. Generally, at the conclusion of the meeting, both parties agree to goals for the next review period and an action plan for future career development.

Schedule the Meeting:

The meeting should be scheduled two to four weeks in advance, at a time convenient and non-disruptive for both parties. The meeting should be held in an office or conference room that's free from distractions.

Employee Ratings:

Consider providing a copy of the performance appraisal form to the employee (without the supervisor's ratings) and ask that the employee evaluate their own performance in each of the respective areas. This can help foster a two-way conversation during the meeting and can help the supervisor focus on the employee's accomplishments. The employee should use the same standards as their supervisor.

Supervisor Ratings:

Use a standardized performance appraisal form to rate employees on assigned targets. An example of a 1-5 rating system is shown here:

5

Exceptional

Consistently and significantly exceeds expectations

4

Exceeds Expectations

Consistently meets expectations and frequently exceeds expectations

3

Meets Expectations

Regularly meets expectations

2

Improvement Needed

Below expectations; although shows some progress in achieving goals

1

Unsatisfactory

Development needs identified; significantly below expectations

When rating the employee, determine how successfully the employee is performing each of their job requirements and whether they met assigned goals. Make sure you can appraise employee performance for each standard or objective. Each rating should be accompanied by detailed comments and examples to support the rating.
