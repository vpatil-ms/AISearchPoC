Overview

The Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) grants employees temporary unpaid, job-protected leave for specific family and medical related reasons. Generally, eligible employees can take leave for a serious health condition, the birth or adoption of a child, and for certain military reasons. The FMLA covers all public employers as well as private employers with 50 or more employees for each working day during 20 or more calendar workweeks in the current or preceding calendar year.

Many states have similar family and medical leave laws, some of which grant employees additional protections for employees and/or cover smaller employers. Where federal and state law overlap, the law with the greatest protection for the employee applies.
