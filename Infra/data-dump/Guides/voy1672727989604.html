I-9 Forms must be kept on file for at least as long as is required by law. Employers are also subject to strict electronic recordkeeping requirements if they choose to retain the forms electronically.

Retention Period:

Completed I-9 Forms must be retained for three years following an employee's date of hire or for one year following their separation from the company, whichever is later. Only the pages of the I-9 Form on which you or the employee enter data must be retained.

Retention Guidelines:

It is a best practice to store all I-9 Forms together in one file since they must be produced promptly following an official request. You may retain completed I-9 Forms on-site or at an off-site storage facility, as long as you are able to present the forms within three days of an inspection request from an officer of the Department of Homeland Security, Office of Special Counsel, or U.S. Department of Labor. Note: I-9 Forms should never be kept in an employee's personnel file.

Electronic Retention of I-9s:

Employers are permitted to complete and retain I-9 Forms in electronic format, as long as certain procedures are followed. If you choose to keep electronic copies of the I-9 Form, you must ensure the system includes:

  • Reasonable controls to ensure its integrity, accuracy, and reliability;
  • Reasonable controls designed to prevent and detect the unauthorized or accidental creation of, addition to, alteration of, deletion of, or deterioration of an electronically completed or stored Form I-9, including the electronic signature, if used;
  • An inspection and quality assurance program that regularly evaluates the system and includes periodic checks of electronically stored Form I-9s, including the electronic signature, if used;
  • An indexing system that allows users to identify and retrieve records maintained in the system;
  • The ability to reproduce legible and readable paper copies; and
  • The ability to produce an “audit trail” for each I-9 that meets DHS standards.

Completing the Form (Electronic Retention):

  • Allow individuals to acknowledge that they read the attestation;
  • Attach the electronic signature to an electronically completed Form I-9;
  • Affix the electronic signature at the time of the transaction;
  • Create and preserve a record verifying the identity of the person producing the signature;
  • Upon the employee's request, provide a printed confirmation of the transaction; and
  • Include a method to acknowledge you have attested to the required information in Section 2.
