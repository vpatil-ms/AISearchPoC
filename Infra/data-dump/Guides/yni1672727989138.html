Documents that Expire Soon:

Generally, if a supporting document contains an expiration date, you must re-verify employment eligibility on or before the date of expiration by completing Section 3 of the I-9 Form. During this re-verification process, you must accept whichever document(s) an employee chooses to present (as long as they are from the Form I-9's acceptable documents list), whether or not they are the same documents provided initially. If the employee is not able to provide proof of current employment eligibility, you cannot continue to employ them.

The following documents may NOT be subject to re-verification:

  • Expired U.S. passport or passport card;
  • Alien Registration Receipt Card/Permanent Resident Card (Form I-551); or
  • List B document that has expired.

Note: Complete Section 3 of the employee's original Form I-9. If you already used Section 3 for a previous re-verification or update, use Section 3 of a new Form I-9 and attach it to the employee's original I-9. You must also complete Section 3 of a new I-9 if the form you used for the previous verification is no longer valid.

See the USCIS's Employer's Handbook for more information.

Rehiring Employees:

You may also be required to complete Section 3 when rehiring a former employee, depending on how much time has passed. If it's been less than three years since the employee initially completed the I-9 Form, you must re-verify their employment eligibility by completing this section of the Form. If the employee's original form of work authorization has expired, you will need to examine a document that reflects current employment eligibility and record the document title, number, and expiration date within this section. If more than three years have passed since the individual initially completed the I-9 Form, they must complete a new I-9.

Data Changes:

Although not required, the USCIS recommends that you note name changes in Section 3 of the I-9 Form. Note: If you use E-Verify, the USCIS also recommends that you encourage your employees to record their legal name change with the Social Security Administration to avoid mismatches in E-Verify.
