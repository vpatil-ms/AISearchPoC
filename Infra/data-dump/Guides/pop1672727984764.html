Pay Ranges

Upon determining internal and external equity, the employer may begin to develop a system for organizing jobs into groups and establishing pay ranges for those groups. A pay range is the minimum, midpoint, and maximum pay for the job grade.

The minimum and maximum of a pay range are determined by adding or subtracting from the midpoint the spread desired by the employer. The typical spread is 15 to 30 percent, depending on the job.

For example, if a job grade has a midpoint of $40,000 and a 15-percent spread, the minimum in the pay range would be $34,000 (85 percent of $40,000), and the maximum would be $46,000 (115 percent of $40,000).

Note: Several states and local jurisdictions require private sector employers to disclose the pay range for a position to an applicant or employee. Check your state and local laws for details.
