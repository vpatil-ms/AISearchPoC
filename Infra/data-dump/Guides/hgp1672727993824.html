Extending an Offer

Once you have completed your interviews, review interview notes and rank candidates based on their qualifications. During the decision-making process consider:

  • Consulting others for input. Meet with everyone who interviewed the applicant and discuss pros/cons and any other feedback they can provide.
  • Ask yourself whether each candidate can meet all of the parameters of the position, including salary, location, and start date.
  • Compile all of the material you have gathered on each candidate: resumes, references, assessments, and interview notes. Compare each candidate's material with the requirements of the job and compare final candidates with one another.
  • Extend a written offer to the most qualified candidate, contingent on any other necessary pre-employment steps, such as a background check, drug test, or reference check. It's best to wait to conduct these tests until after you have extended a conditional offer.

Some employers choose to have an HR representative extend the initial offer via phone in order to negotiate certain key points. This phone call should be followed immediately by an official offer letter that outlines:

  • Job title
  • Pay
  • Start date
  • Supervisor
  • Brief summary of benefits
  • Employment at-will relationship (recognized in all states except Montana)
  • The contingent nature of the offer (e.g., subject to completion of a background check)
