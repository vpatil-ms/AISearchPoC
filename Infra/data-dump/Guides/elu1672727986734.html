Form I-9 Document Receipts

In certain circumstances, employers must accept a receipt in lieu of a List A, List B, or a List C document if one is presented by an employee. Receipts are only valid for a short period of time and only in the following three circumstances:

  • A receipt for a replacement of a lost, stolen, or damaged document. An employee may present a receipt for the application for the replacement of any List A, B, or C document. This receipt is valid for 90 days. When it expires, the employee must show you the replacement document.
  • The arrival portion of Form I-94 with a temporary I-551 stamp and a photograph of the individual. A lawful permanent resident may present this List A receipt instead of showing their Permanent Resident Card (or Form I-551). This receipt is valid until the expiration date on the stamp, or one year after the issuance date if the stamp does not contain an expiration date. When it expires, the employee must show you their Permanent Resident Card (Form I-551).
  • The departure portion of Form I-94 with a refugee admission stamp. A refugee may present this List A receipt to show evidence of both identity and employment authorization. This receipt is valid for 90 days. When the receipt expires, the employee must show you either an Employment Authorization Document (Form I-766) or a combination of a List B document and an unrestricted social security card.

When the employee provides an acceptable receipt, record the document title and number in Section 2 and write the word “receipt” along with the last day that the receipt is valid. After the receipt expires, cross out the word “receipt” and any accompanying document number, record the number and other required document information from the actual document presented, and initial and date the change.
