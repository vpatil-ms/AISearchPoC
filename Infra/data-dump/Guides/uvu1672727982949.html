Discussing Performance Regularly

After performance goals are set, provide employees with frequent, ongoing performance feedback. While formal reviews are important, they shouldn't be the first time the employee has heard from you about how they are doing. Delivering timely positive feedback lets employees know where they are succeeding. This can be extremely motivating and can help employees maintain momentum in reaching their assigned goals. Alternatively, if an employee is falling short, having prompt discussions can help minimize surprises down the road and help correct performance issues before they become bigger problems. When delivering feedback, cite examples and refer to a specific situation or course of conduct.
