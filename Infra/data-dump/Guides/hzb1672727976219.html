Benefits Overview

Employee benefit plans are employer-sponsored programs designed to offer employees a measurable benefit that might not otherwise be affordable or available to the employee. Generally, employers offer employee benefits to demonstrate their commitment to their employees, attract and retain a talented workforce, and meet the security and welfare needs of their employees.

While employers are free to choose whether or not to offer certain benefits like retirement plans and paid time off, employers that do provide their employees with these types of benefits must adhere to certain guidelines. Laws such as the Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA), the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA), and the Affordable Care Act (ACA) place certain requirements on employers and plan administrators.
