Implementing an Employee Handbook

Never assume that your employees will become familiar with your company handbook on their own. Demonstrate the importance of the employee handbook as a communication tool by setting aside some time to discuss it with your employees and answer any questions they may have.

Depending on the size of your company, you may want to meet with supervisors first to educate them on the policies and how to enforce them. After you are confident that supervisors understand the ins and outs of each policy, have them meet with their employees to go over the handbook and answer any of their questions. Additionally, a review of the handbook should be incorporated into your new hire orientation process.
