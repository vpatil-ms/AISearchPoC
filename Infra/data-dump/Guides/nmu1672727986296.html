Handbook Disclaimers

All handbooks should include a clear and conspicuous disclaimer written in large, boldface font at the beginning of the handbook. The disclaimer explains that the handbook does not create a contract guaranteeing employment, nor do the contents of the handbook alter the employee's at-will status.

Employment-at-will is an employment relationship in which either the employer or the employee is free to terminate at any time, for any reason, as long as the reason is a lawful one. By explicitly defining an employment relationship as at-will, an employer makes a good faith effort to make employees aware of the terms of such a relationship.

To be effective, a disclaimer should inform employees that:

  • Nothing in the handbook is to be construed as a contract.
  • Employment is at-will, and either the employer or the employee may, at any time, terminate the employment relationship with or without cause.
  • A statement indicating who within the company has the authority to change the terms of the at-will relationship.
  • Any changes to the at-will relationship must be in writing.
