California law also requires employers employing 26 or more employees to provide supplemental paid sick leave to their covered California employees who are unable to work due to certain reasons related to COVID-19. The law is effective February 19, 2022 and applies retroactively beginning January 1, 2022 through September 30, 2022. The law also requires pay statements for California employees to reflect the total amount of supplemental paid sick leave taken by the employee. If you are not displaying the required information above on employee pay statements then you should ensure this information is provided in a separate written document and distributed each pay period with the employee's wage statement. This sample form can be used for this purpose.
