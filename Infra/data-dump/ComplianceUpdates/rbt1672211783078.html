The city of Montebello (CA) has approved an ordinance that requires certain grocers and drug stores to provide an additional $4 per hour in hazard pay to employees. The requirement will remain in effect until at least July 26, 2021.

Employer Coverage:

To be covered by the ordinance, a grocery or drug store must employ more than 300 grocery or drug store workers nationally and more than 15 employees per grocery or drug store in the city.

Employee Coverage:

For the purposes of the ordinance, covered grocery or drug store workers are limited to those who perform work in Montebello and do not include managers, supervisors, or confidential employees.

Hazard Pay:

The ordinance requires covered employers to provide at least $4 per hour in “premium pay” to covered workers. Premium pay is defined as compensation owed to a grocery or drug store worker that is separate from payments for providing services, bonuses, and commissions, as well as tips earned from customers.

Employers are prohibited reducing a covered employee's regular pay as a result of the ordinance.

Employer Notice:

Employers must provide covered employees with a written notice of rights established by the ordinance. The notice must provide information on:

  • The right to premium pay guaranteed by the ordinance;
  • The right to be protected from retaliation for exercising in good faith the rights protected by the ordinance; and
  • The right to bring a civil action for a violation of the ordinance, including the employer's denial of premium pay and retaliation against an individual for asserting their right to premium pay or otherwise engaging in an activity protected by the ordinance.

The notice must be posted in a location used by employees for breaks, and in an electronic format that is readily accessible to workers. The notice must be made available to employees via smartphone application or an online web portal, in English and any language that the employer knows or has reason to know is the primary language of the worker.

Recordkeeping:

Covered employers must retain records that document compliance with the ordinance for at least two years.

Compliance Recommendations:

Covered employers should ensure compliance with the premium pay, notice, and recordkeeping requirements.
