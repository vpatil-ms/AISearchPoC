Colorado's minimum wage will increase to $12.32 per hour on January 1, 2021. The change is a result of an annual adjustment for inflation.

Tipped Employees:

The minimum direct cash wage for tipped employees will increase to $9.30 per hour on January 1, 2021. If a tipped employee's cash wages and tips fail to meet or exceed $12.32 per hour, employers must make up the difference.

Compliance Recommendations:

Effective January 1, 2021, Colorado employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least $12.32 per hour. Colorado employers must also post an updated Colorado Overtime and Minimum Pay Standards notice. Once published, the state's updated poster will be available for download in the State & Federal Resources section of HR411®.
