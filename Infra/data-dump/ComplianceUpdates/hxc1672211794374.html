The State of Washington will increase its minimum wage on January 1, 2022.

Non-exempt Employees:

On January 1, 2022, the minimum wage will increase to $14.49 for non-exempt employees. All non-exempt employees must be paid at least the minimum wage per hour, but employers may pay 85 percent of the minimum wage (or $12.32) per hour to workers ages 14-15.

Note: Employers are prohibited from using tip credits to pay tipped employees.

Exempt Employees:

Washington's minimum salary threshold to be exempt from overtime (a multiple of the minimum wage for a 40-hour workweek) is set to increase in 2022. This will generally require exempt employees, who satisfy certain job duties tests, to earn a salary of at least 1.75 times the minimum wage ($1,014.30 a week) to be eligible for an overtime exemption.

Note: Employers in Washington may pay computer professionals by the hour (if at least $50.72 per hour) or on a salary basis (if at least $1014.30 per week) for those employees to qualify for an overtime exemption.

Compliance Recommendations:

Employers in Washington State must comply with the new minimum wage and post an updated minimum wage poster in their workplace. Note that certain cities, such as SeaTac and Seattle, may have higher minimum wage rates. When an employee is covered by more than one rate, typically the rate most generous to the employee applies.

Employers must ensure that their exempt workers' salaries meet the new minimum and that these workers otherwise qualify for the exemption by meeting all applicable salary and duties tests. Otherwise, employers must classify these employees as non-exempt and pay overtime when due.
