Rhode Island has enacted legislation (House Bill 5130A) that will raise the state's minimum wage in phases, eventually reaching $15 per hour.

Minimum Wage Schedule:

The minimum wage will increase as follows:

  • $12.25 per hour on January 1, 2022
  • $13.00 per hour on January 1, 2023
  • $14.00 per hour on January 1, 2024
  • $15.00 per hour on January 1, 2025

Tipped employees:

The cash minimum wage for tipped employees will remain at $3.89 per hour. Employers must ensure that tipped employees' direct cash wages plus tips equal or exceed the minimum wage per hour.

Compliance Recommendations:

Rhode Island employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least the minimum wage and display a poster reflecting the new minimum wage. Once published by state, the updated poster will be available for download in the State & Federal Resources section of HR411®.
