The City and County of Santa Fe, New Mexico have announced their minimum wages will increase to $14.03 per hour. The increases take effect on March 1, 2023.

The Details

As of March 1, 2023, employers in the City and County of Santa Fe, New Mexico must pay workers at least $14.03 per hour. See the chart below for additional information and requirements.

 

City of Santa Fe

County of Santa Fe

Covered Employers
  • Employers that are required to have a business license or registration from the city
  • Employers located throughout Santa Fe County, outside of the incorporated boundaries of the City of Santa Fe, City of Española and Town of Edgewood

Covered Workers
  • Employees of a covered employer that are paid through tips, salary, or a combination of both
  • Full-time, part-time or temporary basis workers;
  • Contingent or contracted workers; and
  • Temporary service or employment agency workers

See the county website for exemptions.

Notice Requirements

Employers must post and display the Living Wage notice in English and Spanish in a prominent location on their premises next to their business license or registration.

Employers must post the Living Wage notice in English and Spanish in a prominent location next to the business license.

Tipped Workers

Those customarily receiving more than $100 per month in tips and commissions must be paid a $3.00 per hour minimum cash wage.

Those customarily receiving more than$30 per month in tips must receive a $4.21 per hour minimum cash wage.

Next Steps

As of March 1, 2023, employers must pay at least:

  • $14.03 per hour to non-tipped employees for all hours worked.
  • $3.00 per hour in cash wages to tipped employees in the City of Santa Fe.
  • $4.21 per hour in cash wages to tipped employees in Santa Fe County.

Note: If a worker's tips are not sufficient to meet the minimum wage requirements, the employer must make up the difference.
