The state of Washington has recently issued an emergency rule to provide increased protection for employees exposed to extreme heat.

Overview:

The new regulations are in addition to requirements under the existing Outdoor Heat Exposure rule , which is in place annually from May through September. The existing rules already require ready access to at least one quart of drinking water per worker per hour, an outdoor heat exposure safety program with training, and an appropriate response to workers who are experiencing heat-related illness symptoms.

Temperatures of 100 degrees or more:

The new rule requires that when the temperature is at or above 100 degrees, employers must respond to the extreme heat by:

  • Providing shade or another sufficient means for employees to cool down; and
  • Ensuring workers have a paid cool-down rest of at least 10 minutes every two hours.

Temperatures of 89 degrees or more:

When the temperature is at or above 89 degrees, the new rules, combined with existing rules require employers to:

  • Provide water that is cool enough to drink safely;
  • Allow and encourage workers to take additional paid preventative cool-down rest to protect from overheating;
  • Be prepared by having a written outdoor heat exposure safety program and providing training to employees; and
  • Respond appropriately to any employee with symptoms of heat-related illness.

Compliance Recommendation:

Washington plans to go through the formal rulemaking process to make the emergency rule permanent. Employers should see the Department of Labor &Industries' Be Heat Smart website for additional guidance and update their policies, procedures, and supervisor training to ensure compliance with the emergency rule.
