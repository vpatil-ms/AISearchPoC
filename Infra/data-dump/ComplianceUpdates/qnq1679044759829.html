New York has enacted legislation, Assembly Bill A8092B that further protects workers from punishment or discipline for a lawful absence. Assembly Bill A8092B takes effect on February 19, 2023.

The Details:

Assembly Bill A8092B clarifies employer actions surrounding workplace policies, such as those that are used to control employee absences.

Under the law, New York employers are prohibited from disciplining an employee who takes legally protected time off from work. Employers cannot assess a demerit, occurrence, or any other point or deduction from that employee's allotted bank of time, which could subject them to disciplinary action, such as the failure to receive a promotion or loss of pay.

Next Steps:

New York employers should review their attendance policies and practices to ensure compliance with Assembly Bill A8092B.
