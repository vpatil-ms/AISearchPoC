New York has enacted legislation (Assembly Bill 430), which requires private employers that monitor electronic communications to provide advance written notice on the types of electronic monitoring that may occur. Assembly Bill 430 takes effect on May 7, 2022.

Assembly Bill 430:

The law requires private employers who monitor or otherwise intercept certain electronic communications to provide written notice, which may be in electronic form, to all employees, informing them that they are subject to electronic monitoring. Employees must acknowledge the notice, in writing or electronically, and employers should retain the acknowledgment to demonstrate compliance with the law.

Under Assembly Bill 430, employers must:

  • Provide the notice to employees upon hire;
  • Post the notice in a conspicuous place, which is readily accessible to employees who are subject to electronic monitoring;
  • Inform employees that any and all telephone conversations or transmissions, electronic mail or transmissions, or internet access or usage by an employee by any electronic device or system, including but not limited to the use of a computer, telephone, wire, radio or electromagnetic, photoelectronic or photo-optical systems may be subject to monitoring at any and all times and by any lawful means.

Exceptions:

Under the Act, these provisions do not apply to processes that are designed to manage electronic mail or telephone voice mail or internet usage, that are not targeted to monitor or intercept the electronic mail or telephone voice mail or internet usage of a particular individual, and that are performed solely for computer system maintenance and/or protection.

Enforcement:

Employers found to be in violation of the law may be subject penalties.

Compliance Recommendations:

New York employers should provide the required notice and retain employee acknowledgements to help ensure compliance with Assembly Bill 430.
