New Mexico's minimum wage will increase to $12.00 per hour on January 1, 2023.

Youth Minimum Wage:

The minimum wage for students enrolled in secondary school remains $8.50 per hour, unless the student is a tipped employee (see below).

Tipped Employees:

The minimum cash wage that employers must pay tipped employees (those who customarily and regularly receive more than $30 a month in tips) will increase to $3.00 per hour on January 1, 2023.

If the employee's cash wages and tips don't equal or exceed the applicable minimum wage (e.g., $12.00 per hour in 2023), the employer must make up the difference. The law also requires that tipped employees retain all tips.

Note: The law doesn't prohibit tip pooling among “wait staff” or define wait staff.

Next Steps:

New Mexico employers should ensure compliance with the minimum wage requirements, and once published by the state, post an updated minimum wage poster.
