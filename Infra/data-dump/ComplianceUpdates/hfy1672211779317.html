Vermont's minimum wage will increase to $11.75 per hour, beginning January 1, 2021.

Minimum Wage Increases:

Vermont's minimum wage will increase in phases as outlined below:

Employee Type

2022 rate/hour

2023 and beyond

Non-exempt employees

$12.55

The minimum wage will increase based on inflation

Tipped Employees:

Service and tipped employees (those who customarily and regularly receive more than $120 per month in tips) must receive a direct cash wage of at least half the applicable minimum wage. If the employee's direct cash wages plus tips do not equal or exceed the state minimum wage, the employer must make up the difference.

Compliance Recommendations:

Beginning January 1, 2021, Vermont employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least $11.75 per hour. Vermont employers must also post an updated minimum wage notice in a conspicuous area of the workplace. Once published by the state, the 2021 minimum wage notice will be available for download from the State & Federal Resources section of HR411®.
