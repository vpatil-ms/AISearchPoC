Over 71 percent of the voters in Washington D.C. voted in favor of Measure 82, which phases out the tipped employee credit that employers are currently allowed to take when paying tipped employees. The tip credit will be phased out by 2027.

The Details:

Under current law, D.C. employers of “tipped workers” may take a credit of $5.05 against tipped wages received by workers to satisfy the minimum wage guaranteed to all workers by law.

Measure 82 phases out the tipped credit by increasing the required cash wage that tipped employees must be paid until the cash wage meets parity with the D.C. minimum wage. See the phase out schedule below.

Minimum cash wage paid to tipped employees must be at least the D.C. minimum wage required to be paid to non-tipped employees.

A similar measure passed in 2018 on a 55 percent vote, but was repealed by the D.C. Council in November of 2022. It has been reported that the Council has no intention of repealing its decision from November. ADP will continue monitoring for updates.

Next Steps:

D.C. employers must increase the minimum cash wage paid to tipped employees to at least the amounts noted above on the scheduled dates.
