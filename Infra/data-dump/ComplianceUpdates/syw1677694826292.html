Vermont has enacted legislation (House Bill 729) that limits the time an employee may file a discrimination claim to six years. House Bill 729 is effective immediately.

The Details:

Vermont's Fair Employment Law prohibits employers from discriminating on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, ancestry, place of birth, age, crime victim status, or physical or mental condition.

House Bill 729 limits the period of time that an individuals may file a discrimination claim to six years.

Next Steps:

Vermont employers should update their employer relation policies and practices to ensure compliance with House Bill 729.
