The city of San Jose (CA) has approved an ordinance that will require certain grocers to provide at least $3 per hour in hazard pay to employees. The requirement is in effect from March 26, 2021 through July 24, 2021.

Covered Employers:

To be covered by the ordinance, a grocery store must employ 300 or more employees nationwide, regardless of where those employees are employed, including but not limited to chains, integrated enterprises, or franchises associated with a franchisor or network of franchises with franchisees that employ more than 300 employees in the aggregate.

Covered Employees:

The ordinance covers employees who, in a calendar week, perform at least two hours of work for a covered employer within the geographic boundaries of the city and qualify as an employee entitled to the minimum wage under state law.

Hazard Pay:

In addition to the covered employee's base wage or holiday premium wage, employers must pay covered employees $3 per hour in hazard pay.

Employers are prohibited from taking the following actions as a result of the ordinance:

  • Reducing a covered employee's compensation.
  • Limiting a covered employee's earning capacity.

Notice Requirements:

Covered employers must post in a conspicuous place, at any workplace or job site where any covered employee works, a notice informing employees of their rights under the ordinance. The notice must be posted in English, Spanish, and any other language spoken by at least five percent of the covered employees at the workplace or job site.

On March 26, 2021 or at the time of hire, whichever is later, employers must also provide each covered employee with the covered employer and owner or manager's:

  • Name;
  • Address;
  • Telephone number; and
  • Whether it is part of a chain, integrated enterprise, or franchise associated with a franchisor or network of franchises.

Compliance Recommendations:

Covered employers should ensure compliance with the hazard pay and notice requirements.
