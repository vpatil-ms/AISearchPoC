New Jersey's minimum wage will increase to $12.00 per hour, beginning January 1, 2021.

2021 Minimum Wage:

Employee Type

2021 rate/hour

Non-exempt employees

$12.00

Tipped employees (If the employee's direct cash wages and tips don't equal or exceed the applicable minimum wage, the employer must make up the difference)

$4.13

Small employers (less than six employees) and seasonal workers

$11.10

Training Wage:

The law permits employers to pay a training wage of 90 percent of the general minimum wage (or $10.80 per hour in 2021) to an employee who is enrolled in on-the-job or other training that meets standards set by the state. The training wage may be paid for the first 120 hours of work in an occupation in which the employee has no previous similar or related experience.

Compliance Recommendations:

New Jersey employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least the minimum wage and post the state's Wage & Hour Law Abstract poster in a conspicuous area of the workplace. The poster is available for download from the State & Federal Resources section of HR411®.
