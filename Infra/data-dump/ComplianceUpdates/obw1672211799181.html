South Dakota has enacted legislation (Senate Bill 5) that amends and adds tighter restrictions to its state medical marijuana law. Senate Bill 5 takes effect on July 1, 2022.

Background:

Current Provisions:

South Dakota law allows employers to prohibit employees from using cannabis at work and from working while under its influence.

The law also contains carveout provisions for medical marijuana users. Under the provisions, employers cannot consider a worker to be under the influence solely because a drug test detects metabolites or components of cannabis if the amounts detected are too small to cause impairment.

Definitions:

A “Safety-sensitive job” is a position with tasks or duties that an employer reasonably believes could cause the illness, injury, or death of a person or result in serious property damage.

“Under the influence” is an abnormal mental or physical condition that tends to deprive a person of clearness of intellect and control that they would otherwise possess due to the consumption of any degree of cannabis or cannabis products.

The Details:

Senate Bill 5 removes the cannabis carveouts and allows employers to enforce drug-free workplace policies (including a drug-testing program). Employers may also prohibit the use, display, possession, transfer, and transport of cannabis in any workplace, and may prohibit any employee from working while under the influence.

Senate Bill 5 also prohibits, among other things, the following actions:

· Performing a safety-sensitive job or operating, navigating, or being in actual physical control of any motor vehicle, aircraft, train, or motorboat while under the influence; or

· Undertaking any task under the influence of cannabis, when doing so would constitute negligence or professional malpractice.

Next Steps:

Employers in South Dakota should review their drug testing policies, forms, practices, and supervisor training to ensure compliance with Senate Bill 5.
