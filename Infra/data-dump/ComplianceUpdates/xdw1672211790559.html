Nevada has enacted legislation (Assembly Bill 280) that requires employers to designate single-occupancy restrooms as gender-neutral beginning October 1, 2021.

Overview:

Assembly Bill 280 requires that all single-occupancy bathroom facilities located in public places (including those located in hotels, inns, motels, restaurants, bars, gas stations, theaters, bakeries, grocery stores, daycare or senior citizen centers, gyms, and spas) be inclusive and accessible to a person of any gender identity or expression. This means the restroom must allow a:

  • Parent or guardian of a child to enter the single-stall restroom with the child;
  • Person with a disability to enter the single-stall restroom with their caregiver; and
  • Person of any gender identity or expression to use the single-stall restroom as needed.

Signage:

Business owners may label the single-stall restroom as available for use by anyone, including posting a sign that reads “All-Gender Bathroom” or “All-Accessible Bathroom.” The law prohibits the use of gendered signage, which includes any sign posted on a single-stall restroom that uses words or images of a person to designate sex.

Compliance Recommendations:

Covered employers should review their policies and practices to ensure compliance with Assembly Bill 280.
