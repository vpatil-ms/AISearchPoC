The Colorado Department of Labor and Employment (CDLE) has adopted a rule that will, among other things, increase the minimum salary required to be considered exempt from overtime under state law.

Background:

Under state law, employers must pay non-exempt employees at least the minimum wage for each hour worked. These employees are also entitled to 1.5 times their regular rate for all hours in excess of: (1) 40 hours in a workweek; (2) 12 hours in a workday; or (3) 12 consecutive hours without regard to starting and ending time of the workday, whichever calculation results in the greatest payment of wages. There are exemptions from these requirements for bona fide executive/supervisor, administrative, and professional employees who meet certain salary and duties tests.

COMPS Order #36:

The CDLE has adopted a rule that will replace Minimum Wage Order #35 with Colorado Overtime and Minimum Pay Standards Order (“COMPS Order”) #36. Among other things, COMPS Order #36 expands the industries covered by the order as well as increases the minimum salary required to be considered exempt under state law.

Covered Industries:

COMPS Order #36 modifies coverage from four broadly defined industries (Retail and Service, Food and Beverage, Commercial Support Service, and Health and Medical) to cover all employees, unless specifically exempted. This change is effective March 16, 2020.

Minimum Salary Requirements:

The minimum salary required to qualify for the executive/supervisor, administrative, and professional exemptions under state law will increase as follows:

Beginning January 1, 2025, the weekly salary requirement will be adjusted annually for inflation.

Remember, employees must also satisfy the applicable state duties test to be considered exempt from overtime under state law.

Exception:

The state's 2020 salary increase doesn't apply to the following two categories of employers:

  • Non-profit employers with annual total gross revenue of less than $50 million.
  • For-profit employers with annual total gross revenue of less than $1 million.

These employers will be subject to the increases in 2021 and beyond.

Computer Professional Employee Exemption:

Colorado also has an exemption for highly technical computer occupations. COMPS Order #36 makes changes to the tests for this exemption. See the text of the order for details.

Handbook Requirements:

In addition to posting COMPS Order #36 in the workplace, employers that publish or distribute a handbook or written policies must include a copy of the order, or a poster published by the state, with their handbook or policies.

If employers require employees to sign a handbook, manual, or policy, they must have the employee sign an acknowledgement that they received the order or poster.

Other Changes:

COMPS Order #36 makes clarifications on various other topics, including meal period and rest breaks, deductions, and prohibitions on retaliation. Additionally, employers are no longer allowed to require a security deposit for a uniform.

Compliance Recommendations:

Colorado employers should review COMPS Order #36 in full and ensure compliance with all provisions.
