New York has enacted legislation (Senate Bill 9427A), which requires employers to disclose compensation or the range of compensation to applicants and employees. Senate Bill 9427A takes effect September 17, 2023.

The Details:

Definitions:

Under Senate Bill 9427A, the range of compensation is defined as the minimum and maximum annual salary or hourly range of compensation for a job, promotion or transfer opportunity that the employer, in good faith, believes to be accurate at the time of the posting of an advertisement for such opportunity.

The law also defines an employer as any person, corporation, limited liability company, association, labor organization or entity employing four or more employees in any occupation, industry, trade, business or service, or any agent thereof; and any person, corporation, limited liability company, association or entity acting as an employment agent or recruiter, or otherwise connecting applicants with employers.

Note: The law does not include temporary help firms as employers.

Pay disclosure:

Employers must disclose the compensation or a range of compensation and the job description (if it exists) for each job, promotion or transfer opportunity, when advertising for such a position that can or will be performed, at least in part, in New York.

Note: Advertisements for jobs, promotions or transfer opportunities paid solely on commission must disclose a written general statement that compensation will be based on commission.

Nonretaliation:

Employers are prohibited from refusing to interview, hire, promote or employ, or retaliating against an applicant or current employee for exercising any rights under the law.

Penalties:

Employers that are found to have failed to comply with a requirement under the law may face a civil penalty.

Recordkeeping:

New York employers must keep and maintain necessary records to comply with the requirements under the law. This includes the history of compensation ranges for each job, promotion or transfer opportunity, and the job description for such a position (if it exists).

Next Steps:

  • Review advertisements for jobs, promotions or transfer opportunities to help ensure compliance with Senate Bill 9427A by September 17, 2023.
  • New York City employers should coordinate compliance with the city's pay transparency law.
  • Be on the lookout for updated rules and regulations from the state, which will be made available on the state website.
