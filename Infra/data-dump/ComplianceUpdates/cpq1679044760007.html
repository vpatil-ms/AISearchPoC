The minimum wage, hourly cash wage and tip credits, and minimum salary threshold will increase in certain New York jurisdictions. These changes take effect on December 31, 2022.

The Details:

Exempt employee salary requirements:

The minimum salary New York employers can pay to exempt administrative and executive workers remains at $1,125 for employers in New York City, Westchester County, and Long Island, and it increases to $1,064.25 for the remainder of upstate New York on December 31, 2022.

Next Steps:

New York employers should ensure compliance with the following by December 31, 2022:

  • The minimum wage requirements for hourly workers
  • State rules for classifying and paying exempt employees

Note: Employers with exempt employees should avoid improper deductions and other practices that may jeopardize the employee's exempt status.
