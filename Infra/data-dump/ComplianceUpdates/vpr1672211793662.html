California has enacted legislation (Senate Bill 62) that prohibits employers from paying garment workers by piece-rate. Instead, employers must pay an hourly rate no less than the applicable minimum wage or face a fine of $200 per employee, per pay period. Senate Bill 62 takes effect January 1, 2022.

Note: The prohibition on piece-rate pay for garment workers doesn't apply to worksites covered by a valid collective bargaining agreement. The law also doesn't prohibit incentive-based bonuses.

Other Changes

Senate Bill 62 clarifies that garment manufacturers, brand guarantors and contractors are all liable for all of the following:

  • Unpaid minimum, regular, overtime, and other premium wages, reimbursement for expenses, any other compensation, including interest, due to garment workers;
  • The employee's reasonable attorney's fees and costs;
  • Penalties for failing to obtain valid worker's compensation insurance; and
  • Damages and penalties, including interest, due for violations of the Labor Code.

Recordkeeping:

Senate Bill 62 requires all garment-manufacturing employers to keep records for four years of all contracts, invoices, purchase orders, work orders, style or cut sheets, garment licenses and any other documentation related to garment manufacturing.

“Brand guarantors” must keep accurate records of the following for four years, in addition to any other recordkeeping requirements:

  • Contract worksheets indicating the price per unit agreed to between the brand guarantor and the contractor or manufacturer.
  • All contracts, invoices, purchase orders, work or job orders, and style or cut sheets.
  • A copy of the garment license of every person with whom the employer has entered into a contract for garment manufacturing.

Garment manufacturing is defined as sewing, cutting, making, processing, repairing, finishing, assembling, dyeing, altering a garment's design, affixing a label to a garment, or otherwise preparing any garment or accessory intended to be worn by an individual. See the text of the law for the definition of brand guarantors.

Compliance Recommendations:

Garment manufacturers with employees in California should ensure compliance with Senate Bill 62 by January 1, 2022.
