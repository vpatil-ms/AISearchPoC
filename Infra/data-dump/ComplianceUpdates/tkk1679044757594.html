Nebraska's minimum wage will increase to $10.50 per hour on January 1, 2023. The change is a result of a passed ballot initiative.

Tipped Employees:

Tipped employees must receive direct cash wages of at least $2.13 per hour. If the tipped employee's tips and direct cash wages fail to meet or exceed the applicable minimum wage, the employer must make up the difference.

Training Wage:

Student learners in a bona fide vocational training program must receive at least $7.875 (75 percent of the minimum wage).

Next Steps:

Beginning January 1, 2023, Nebraska employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least the minimum wage per hour. Nebraska employers must also post an updated minimum wage poster in a conspicuous location, once it is posted by the state.
