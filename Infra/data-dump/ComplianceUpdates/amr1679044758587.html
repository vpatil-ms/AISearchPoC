Rhode Island's minimum wage will increase to $13.00 per hour, beginning January 1, 2023.

Tipped Employees:

Beginning January 1, 2023, tipped employees (except those that receive gratuities for taxicabs and limited public motor vehicles) must receive direct cash wages of at least $3.89 per hour. If the tipped employee's tips and direct cash wages fail to meet or exceed the applicable minimum wage, the employer must make up the difference.

Exceptions:

Full-time students under 19 years of age working in nonprofit religious, educational, librarial, or community service organizations, and 14 or 15-year-olds that work 24 or more hours a week, must receive at least $11.70 (90% of the applicable minimum wage).

Note: For any week in which a 14 or 15-year-old works less than 24 hours, the worker must receive $9.75 per hour (75% of the applicable minimum wage) for all hours worked in that week.

Rhode Island has provided guidance on additional exceptions.

Next Steps:

Rhode Island employers should ensure compliance with the minimum wage requirements, and once published by the state, post an updated minimum wage poster.
