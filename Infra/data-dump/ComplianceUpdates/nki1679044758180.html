New Jersey's minimum wage will increase to $14.13 per hour for most employees, beginning January 1, 2023.

2023 Minimum Wage:

Beginning on January 1, 2023, the following changes to the minimum wage will take effect:

  • Employers with more than six employees must pay non-exempt employees at least $14.13 per hour.
  • Employers with fewer than six employees and seasonal employers must pay employees at least $12.93 per hour.

Tipped employees:

The tipped worker's cash wage will increase to $5.26 per hour, with an employer able to claim a tip credit of up to $8.87. If the minimum cash wage plus an employee's tips does not equal at least the state minimum wage, the employer must pay the difference.

Training Wage:

The law permits employers to pay a training wage of $12.717 per hour (90 percent of the general minimum wage in 2023) to an employee who is enrolled in on-the-job or other training that meets standards set by the state. The training wage may be paid for the first 120 hours of work in an occupation in which the employee has no previous similar or related experience.

Next Steps:

New Jersey employers must pay all non-exempt employees at least the minimum wage and post the state's Wage & Hour Law Abstract poster, once it is made available by the state, in a conspicuous area of the workplace.
