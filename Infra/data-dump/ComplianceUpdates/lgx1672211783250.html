The city of Irvine (CA) has approved an ordinance that will require certain retailers to provide at least $4 per hour in hazard pay to grocery workers. The requirement will take effect on March 25, 2021 and will remain in effect through July 23, 2021.

Covered Employers:

To be covered by the ordinance, employers must:

  • Be located in the city;
  • Employ at least 20 employees at the retail establishment;
  • Have an owner, parent company, franchisor, or network of franchises that employs 500 or more employees nationally; and
  • Meet one of the following definitions of a covered retail establishment:
    • At least 15,000 square feet in size and devote 70 percent or more of its sales floor area to retailing a general range of food products, which may be fresh or packaged, or receives 70 percent or more revenue from retailing a general range of food products;
    • More than 85,000 square feet and devote 10 percent or more of its sales floor area to the sale of merchandise that is non-taxable pursuant to Section 6359 of the Revenue and Taxation Code, including retail establishments with multiple tenants, so long as consumer goods and nontaxable items are sold under the same roof with shared checkout stands, entrances, and exits; or
    • A retail pharmacy that sells a variety of prescription and nonprescription medicines, as well as any combination of miscellaneous items, including but not limited to sundries, dry foods, packaged foods, beverages, fresh produce, meats, deli products, dairy products, canned foods, or prepared foods.

Covered Employees:

To be covered by the ordinance, a grocery worker must perform at least two hours of work in a calendar week for a covered employer within the city. The ordinance doesn't apply to managerial, supervisory, or confidential employees of a covered employer.

Hazard Pay:

Employers must pay covered employees at least $4 per hour in hazard pay. The hazard pay must be in addition to the employee's base pay, commissions, gratuities, bonuses or any other form of regular or premium pay that is owed to the grocery worker, including but not limited to any holiday, overtime, or vacation pay.

Employers are prohibited from funding the hazard pay required by reducing the base wage rate, overtime, holiday or other premium pay rate, hours of work, vacation, pension contributions, or other non-wage benefits of any grocery worker, or by increasing charges to any grocery worker for parking, meals, or other work-related materials or equipment.

Notice Requirements:

Covered employers must post a notice informing employees of their rights under the ordinance. Employers must post the notice in a conspicuous location, and in an electronic format that is readily accessible to the grocery workers, including smartphone application or online web portal. The notice must be in English, Spanish, and any other primary language spoken by at least 10 percent of the retail establishment.

Recordkeeping:

Covered employers must retain records that document compliance with the ordinance, including payroll records listing the hazard pay as a separate item, for a period of two years.

Compliance Recommendations:

Covered employers should ensure compliance with the hazard pay and notice requirements.
