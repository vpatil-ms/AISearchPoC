Long Beach (CA) has approved an ordinance that will require certain grocery stores to provide premium pay to employees performing work in the city.

Covered Employers:

To be covered by the ordinance, the grocery store must employ:

  • More than 300 grocery workers nationally; and
  • More than 15 employees per grocery store in the city.

The ordinance defines a grocery store as an entity that devotes at least 70 percent of its business to retailing a general range of food products, which may be fresh or packaged.

Covered Employees:

To be covered by the ordinance, a worker must be employed directly by a covered employer and work within the city. Managers, supervisors, and confidential employees aren't covered by the ordinance.

Hazard Pay:

Covered employers must provide each grocery worker with premium pay consisting of $4.00 per hour for each hour worked. The hazard pay requirement remains in effect until May 22, 2021.

Notice:

Covered employers must provide covered grocery workers with a written notice of rights established by the ordinance. The notice must be posted in break locations and in an electronic format that is readily accessible to workers, including via smartphone app or an online web portal. The notice mush be provided in English and any language that the employer knows or has reason to know is the primary language of the grocery worker(s).

Recordkeeping:

Covered employers must retain records that document compliance with the ordinance for covered workers for a period of two years.

Retaliation Prohibited:

Covered employers are prohibited from discharging, reducing the compensation of, or otherwise discriminating against any grocery worker for opposing any practice prohibited by the ordinance, participating in proceedings related to the ordinance, or seeking to exercise their rights under the ordinance.

Compliance Recommendations:

Covered employers should ensure compliance with the ordinance at least until May 22, 2021.
